Her Story

At 16, Leila lost her cousin Draiven—nicknamed "Benji"—to an overdose in October 2023. He was 19. Draiven was one of five family members Leila has lost to overdose.

When she tried to access Narcan on her own for peace of mind, she found it difficult. That's when she realized: if a motivated teenager couldn't easily get this life-saving medication, how many others were going without?

"Instead of being paralyzed by those questions, I decided to take action," she says. "Two-thirds of overdose deaths happen with bystanders nearby—people who could save a life if they only knew how. I founded Hope Resuscitated to make sure teens have the tools and training they need."

"One life saved is everything, one life saved is a success. Every Narcan kit distributed, every teen trained, every overdose reversed—that's Benji's legacy, and ours."

Learn to Save a Life

Knowledge is power. Understanding the signs and knowing how to respond can make all the difference.

👁️

Recognizing an Overdose

Learn the common signs of an opioid overdose, such as slowed breathing, unresponsiveness, and blue lips or nails. If you notice these signs, it's critical to act immediately.

🚨

How to Respond

Discover what to do if you suspect someone is overdosing, including calling 911, administering Narcan, and performing rescue breathing if needed. Remember, your quick action can save a life.

💡

Preventing Overdoses

Educate yourself and others on reducing the risk of overdose through safe use practices, understanding tolerance, and accessing support for addiction and recovery.

How to Use NARCAN® Nasal Spray

1

CHECK

Is person unresponsive? Yell "Wake up!" and shake gently

2

GIVE

Insert nozzle in nostril and press plunger firmly

3

CALL

Call 911 immediately after first dose

4

WATCH

Wait 2-3 minutes. Give another dose if needed

5

STAY

Stay with person until help arrives

Instructions also available in Spanish at all distribution sites
