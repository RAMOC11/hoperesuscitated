Her Story

At 16, Sophia lost her best friend Alex to an overdose. Alex was brilliant, kind, and loved by everyone — but there was a stigma around his struggles that kept him isolated. At his memorial, Sophia realized that overdose deaths were preventable with the right tools and knowledge, yet thousands of teens went without access.

"I knew I had to do something," she says. "I was angry, heartbroken, but I channeled it into action. I researched naloxone, got certified, and started training at my school. Within months, other students wanted to help. Now, three years later, we've trained over 4,200 teens and distributed 8,500 Narcan kits. Every number represents a potential life saved — and maybe prevents another family from experiencing what mine did."

"I founded Hope Resuscitated not for recognition, but because I refused to let Alex's memory fade. Every teen trained, every Narcan kit distributed, every life saved — that's his legacy, and ours."
